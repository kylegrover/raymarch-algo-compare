The story in one sentence: adaptive tracers win exactly where sphere tracing is forced into many tiny steps — grazing angles and intricate / non-metric surfaces — not where the distance field is merely expensive to evaluate. Smooth surfaces converge in ~10 steps and Standard is unbeatable there.

A non-metric (Lipschitz>1) fractal: the field overshoots true distance, so Standard must creep in tiny safe steps. At equal time it still trails the relaxed stepper — which reaches the quality bar up to ~8× sooner.

Where the baseline still rules

Smooth, low-grazing, well-conditioned distance fields. Here the adaptive methods are often structurally capped — they can't even reach the quality bar — while Standard is both fastest and safest.

  • Standard
    Thin Torus — all three views; every relaxed/segment method is capped on the thin ring.
  • Standard
    Hollow Cube (CSG) — all three views; thin shell walls, only Standard & Safe reach the bar.
  • Standard
    Cube face & grazing-face, Sphere ortho, Onion Shell macro — clean convex geometry, head-on.
  • caveat
    Segment is capped everywhere except the grazing plane — a narrow but real and certifiable win, broken off that regime.
  • caveat
    Naive-Relaxed leads many wall-clock rows but is the naive over-relaxation — fast yet can tunnel thin features. Its wins pass the IoU≥0.98 bar but still warrant a visual gut-check before full trust.
