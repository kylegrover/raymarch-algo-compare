# Sweep findings

_3864 scored runs · 14 scene-views · 9 strategies · budget + matched-residual axes · scored vs the calibrated dense-march oracle._

> Generated by `raymarching_benchmark.report.analyze`. IoU is the primary (objective geometric) metric; cost is reported on three axes (GPU ms, SDF evals/ray, iteration divergence) and never collapsed.

## 0. Headline takeaways

1. **Plain sphere tracing is the baseline that's hard to beat.** Standard wins or
   ties (IoU ≥ 0.99) on almost every well-behaved view, is *never* structurally
   limited, and is among the cheapest in GPU ms. The "advanced" tracers mostly
   buy nothing on accuracy for well-conditioned SDFs — they earn their keep only
   on the planar/grazing case (where over-relaxation and segment tracing shine).

2. **Safe over-relaxation is the one advanced method that's both robust and
   competitive.** Keinert's Safe-Relaxed *never* appears in the structurally-
   limited table, and tuning ω barely moves it (+0.004 IoU, §4) — its predictive
   disjoint-sphere fallback protects it at every ω. Contrast with **Naive
   over-relaxation**, which is fast but *fragile*: it tunnels thin/sharp features
   (Thin-Torus IoU 0.43–0.65, Cube-face 0.82) and only its *least* aggressive
   ω=1.2 avoids catastrophe (tuning gain +0.25). Same idea, opposite reliability —
   exactly why the relabel mattered.

3. **Segment tracing, as implemented, is broken off the plane.** It's the cheapest
   method on the Grazing Plane (its assumptions hold perfectly there) but collapses
   on curvature/thin features — Thin-Torus macro **IoU 0.003**, Cube grazing-face
   0.68, even Sphere 0.85. This is the definitive answer to the old "interval ≡
   baseline" question: not a quiet win, a real bug (its candidate-segment
   extension/bracket mishandles curved & thin geometry). **Top fix candidate.**

4. **Eval-count and GPU wall-clock genuinely disagree.** Enhanced is repeatedly
   the *fewest evals* yet *dearer in ms* (extra per-step ALU + high divergence,
   §5); Safe-Relaxed too. Never rank methods on eval-count alone — §2 lists the
   inversions per scene, §5 the divergence that drives them.

5. **The Mandelbulb (non-metric, L>1) is where trust runs out.** No method cleanly
   reaches the oracle (best ≈ 0.98), and the nominal "winner" there is *Naive*
   over-relaxation — a method we know tunnels — which is a strong hint the
   **dense-march oracle itself is least certain on the fractal**. This is the
   prime target for the Phase 6 interval-arithmetic gold-standard reference; treat
   fractal verdicts as provisional until then.

> Caveat on the "winner" column in §1: where many methods tie at IoU 1.000 (e.g.
> Sphere, Grazing Plane) the winner is just the cheapest of the tie — read the
> "reach ≥0.99" set as the real signal there.
